# Entrepreneurial OS — Savant-Level Skill

## Philosophy

Entrepreneurial mastery = **stage-appropriate decisions** (what works at $0 kills at $1M), **decision velocity over decision perfection** (speed is the moat at early stage), **systems that run without you** (leverage = the only way a solo founder competes), and **protecting the asset** (you are the company — burnout is bankruptcy).

---

## 1. Stage-Gate Model

### The 5 Stages
```
Stage 0 → Exploration    ($0 MRR, pre-idea or pre-validation)
Stage 1 → Validation     ($0–$1K MRR, testing the riskiest assumptions)
Stage 2 → Traction       ($1K–$10K MRR, repeatable distribution found)
Stage 3 → Growth         ($10K–$100K MRR, scaling what works)
Stage 4 → Dominance      ($100K+ MRR, building defensible moats)
Stage 5 → Exit / Legacy  (Acquisition, IPO, or wealth-generating asset)
```

### Stage-Specific Priorities
```
Stage 0 — Exploration:
  ✅ DO: Talk to 20 potential customers. Identify pain > $500/year.
  ✅ DO: Stay curious. Explore 3 ideas in parallel.
  ❌ DON'T: Write code. Build products. Spend money.
  Key metric: Number of validated pains discovered

Stage 1 — Validation:
  ✅ DO: Build the smallest possible thing to prove willingness to pay.
  ✅ DO: Get 3 paying customers before scaling anything.
  ✅ DO: The Mom Test on every call (extract facts, not opinions).
  ❌ DON'T: Hire. Fundraise. Rebrand. Optimize.
  Key metric: First $1 paid by a non-friend

Stage 2 — Traction:
  ✅ DO: Double down on the one channel that's working.
  ✅ DO: Document everything repeatable.
  ✅ DO: Reduce churn below 3% monthly.
  ❌ DON'T: Add features the top 10% of users don't need.
  Key metric: Month-over-month MRR growth rate

Stage 3 — Growth:
  ✅ DO: Build systems and SOPs. Start hiring/contracting.
  ✅ DO: Competitive moat work (switching costs, network effects).
  ✅ DO: Begin building brand and thought leadership.
  ❌ DON'T: Over-engineer. Premature abstraction kills speed.
  Key metric: CAC payback period < 6 months

Stage 4 — Dominance:
  ✅ DO: Category definition. Become the reference brand.
  ✅ DO: Acquire competitors or adjacent assets.
  ✅ DO: Build defensible data or community flywheel.
  Key metric: Market share and NPS vs. competitors
```

## 2. Decision Frameworks

### The OODA Loop (Founder Edition)
```
Observe → Orient → Decide → Act → (loop)

Founder OODA cadence:
  Daily OBSERVE:   What's new? Any signal that changes priorities? (15 min)
  Weekly ORIENT:   Is my mental model of the market still accurate? (30 min)
  Weekly DECIDE:   What's the one experiment to run this week? (30 min)
  Daily ACT:       Batch distraction. Block 4h of deep work. Ship something.

Key insight: Most founders skip Orient. They act on stale assumptions.
Orient = reading, customer calls, data review. Never skip it.
```

### Priority Stack (Solo Founder Edition)
```
Eisenhower Matrix adapted for revenue-stage founders:

Urgent + Important → DO NOW:
  - Paying customer problem
  - Revenue-at-risk issue
  - Blocking technical outage

Not Urgent + Important → SCHEDULE DEEP WORK:
  - Product development
  - Marketing content creation
  - Customer success systems
  - Learning and skill development

Urgent + Not Important → DELEGATE/AUTOMATE:
  - Administrative tasks
  - Repetitive answers to common questions
  - Scheduling, formatting, data entry

Not Urgent + Not Important → ELIMINATE:
  - Social media scrolling
  - Low-signal networking
  - Meetings with no clear outcome

// Solo founder rule: Every "urgent + not important" should become an automation or template within 48h of doing it twice.
```

### Pivot vs. Persist Framework
```
PIVOT signals (2+ = consider pivoting):
  □ 3 consecutive months of zero organic growth
  □ Churn > 8% monthly despite product improvements
  □ 5+ customer interviews show pain is "nice to have," not "must have"
  □ CAC > 3x LTV and improving product doesn't fix it
  □ Founder has lost conviction in the vision

PERSIST signals (2+ = keep going):
  □ NPS > 40 from even a small cohort
  □ Users paying despite rough UX ("teeth gritter" retention)
  □ At least one channel showing sub-$100 CAC
  □ Problem is real and urgent — data confirms it
  □ Your differentiation is real and hard to copy

PIVOT types (from least to most drastic):
  1. Channel pivot: Same product, different distribution
  2. Customer pivot: Same product, different ICP
  3. Feature pivot: Extract one feature as standalone product
  4. Platform pivot: Rebuild for a different platform
  5. Full pivot: New problem, new customer, new product
```

## 3. Weekly Operating Rhythm

### The Solo Founder Week
```
Monday (Planning — 60 min):
  1. Review last week's one metric
  2. Set this week's single most important project (SIP)
  3. Block all distraction for deep work on SIP (Tue-Thu)
  4. Schedule customer calls for this week

Tuesday-Thursday (Deep Work — 4h/day):
  - Only SIP work. Phone silent. Email batched to 30 min/day.
  - Track hours on SIP (target: 12h/week minimum)

Friday (Review + Ship — 2h):
  1. Ship or publish something (product, content, email)
  2. Review: Did SIP advance? What blocked me?
  3. Record 1 learning, 1 win, 1 mistake in founder journal
  4. Update key metrics dashboard

Weekend rule: 1 day fully off per week. Non-negotiable.
```

### Quarterly Strategic Review
```
Q-review questions (90-min solo retreat):
  1. Am I in the right stage? Am I acting accordingly?
  2. What is the single most important constraint right now?
  3. What should I STOP doing?
  4. What would make this quarter 10x better?
  5. Is my financial runway healthy? What's my survival plan?
  6. Have I talked to ≥20 customers this quarter?
  7. What experiments did I run? What did they prove?
  8. What does the next quarter's version of me look like?

Output: 3 quarterly boulders (big rocks, not todos)
```

## 5. Founder Psychology & Energy Management

### The Founder Energy Audit
```
Weekly check (5 min, every Sunday):
  Energy drains (mark each -1 to -5):
    [ ] Meetings with no outcome
    [ ] Context switching
    [ ] Working on things I'm bad at
    [ ] Unclear priorities
    [ ] Unresolved conflicts

  Energy gains (mark each +1 to +5):
    [ ] Shipping something I'm proud of
    [ ] Learning that accelerates progress
    [ ] Customer win / positive feedback
    [ ] Creative deep work
    [ ] Exercise / physical reset

  Rule: Net energy < -5 two weeks in a row = mandatory restructure.
```

### Burnout Prevention Protocol
```
Early warning signs (address immediately):
  - "I don't care if this ships" → Need a win; ship something small
  - Dreading Mondays → Wrong stage priorities; reassess what matters
  - Avoiding customer calls → Fear of rejection; do a call today
  - Doom-scrolling for hours → Overwhelm; single task for 24h

Recovery protocol (before burnout, not after):
  1. Identify the real source (project, relationship, financial stress)
  2. Do one tiny thing that creates momentum (10-minute win)
  3. Take one full day offline — no phone, no laptop
  4. Review: Is the vision still right? Is the price worth paying?
```

## Anti-Patterns

**Anti-Pattern 1: Stage Mismatch — Acting Like a Scale-Up in Validation**
Hiring, rebranding, building complex infrastructure, or setting up detailed processes before you have 10 paying customers. Everything that works at $10M/year is waste at $10K/year. Process before product-market fit is theater.
Fix: Stage 1 operating rules are: do things that don't scale, talk to customers daily, never automate something you don't yet know works. Only add process when the absence of it is causing revenue to leak.

**Anti-Pattern 2: Decision Paralysis via Research**
Spending weeks researching "the perfect stack," "the best niche," or "the right price" instead of running a 48-hour experiment that would answer the question empirically. Founders who research instead of test are optimizing for feeling right instead of being right.
Fix: Set a research timebox (max 4 hours). After the timebox, the only output is a specific experiment to run in the next 5 days. The experiment answer is worth 100x the research answer.

**Anti-Pattern 3: Doing Everything Instead of Multiplying Through Leverage**
A solo founder who is their own designer, accountant, customer support, and content creator is running a job, not a company. The founder's job is to find leverage: AI tools, templates, contractors, automations, and productized services that multiply output.
Fix: Track your hourly rate. If a task would cost <$50 to contract out and you're spending hours on it, you're volunteering to lose money. Every week, identify one task to remove from your plate permanently.

## Quality Gates

- [ ] Current stage correctly identified; acting with stage-appropriate constraints
- [ ] One SIP (single most important project) defined for this week — not a list
- [ ] Weekly rhythm running: plan Monday, deep work Tue-Thu, ship Friday
- [ ] Pivot/persist decision refreshed within last 90 days
- [ ] Founder energy audit done in the last 7 days
- [ ] AI leverage audit done quarterly: every manual task examined for automation

## Failure Modes and Fallbacks

**Failure: Founder stuck in validation purgatory for 6+ months**
Detection: Still "testing ideas," zero revenue, pivoting every 4-6 weeks without shipping.
Fallback: Force a constraint. Pick one idea. Give it 60 days and $0 marketing budget. Build only what is needed to collect $1. If you cannot get one person to pay in 60 days, the problem is the founder's willingness to ask for money, not the product.

**Failure: Solo founder attempts to operate without any systems**
Detection: Every week feels like starting over. Decisions take hours. Same problems recur. Work expands to fill all available time with no visible progress.
Fallback: Do a time audit for one week. Track every hour. Then: delete tasks that don't directly contribute to revenue or retention, templatize recurring decisions, and implement the weekly operating rhythm above with a hard commitment to the Friday ship rule.
